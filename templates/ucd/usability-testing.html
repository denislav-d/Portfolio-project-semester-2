Usability testing

This week our group made the usability test plan, after which, each one of us conducted a usability test. The primary objective of the study was to enhance and identify any shortcomings in our project website prior to submitting its final version. This way, I was able to gain many valuable insights, without taking too much time, which is important to the user, as they can stay focused.

The plan contains information about the whole procedure - business case, product under test, objectives, responsibilities, participants and their tasks. Despite the short length of this type of prototype testing, it rewarded us with a lot of useful feedback.

The session took place in a room where the I posed questions to the interviewee, centered around exploring the website, performing specific tasks, and providing an overall opinion of the website. I also documented the proceedings and posed additional questions to gather additional information. Ultimately, the testing process was successful, as the interviewee completed all the navigation tasks and offered feedback on areas that could be enhanced or made more user-friendly.
